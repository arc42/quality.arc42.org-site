# LinkedIn Series Plan: q42 Recent Evolution (Aug 2025 - Feb 2026)

## Objective

Explain the most relevant recent q42 changes to a broad software-engineering audience, with emphasis on practical value:

- better navigation and discoverability
- clearer terminology
- stronger requirements quality
- stronger standards integration

Also reinforce that **q42 is open source** and part of the broader **arc42 family**.

## Suggested Series Structure (9 Posts)

1. **Why q42 evolved now**
2. **From "properties" to "dimensions"**
3. **Graph UX improvements for daily use**
4. **New homepage and navigation paths**
5. **Aliases + canonical language**
6. **Requirements quality upgrade**
7. **Standards expansion (5 -> 35)**
8. **Standards explorer + category browsing**
9. **Open source roadmap and call for contributions**

## Cadence

- 2 posts/week over ~4-5 weeks
- Publish Tue/Thu (or Tue/Fri) for consistency
- Reuse one visual per post (graph screenshot, standards grid, aliases page, etc.)

## Cross-cutting messages to "sprinkle in"

- q42 is maintained in the open, with transparent history and community review.
- q42 is not a standalone island: it is part of the **arc42 ecosystem**.
- q42 connects architecture communication (arc42) with measurable quality and concrete requirements.
- The model is evolving with modern concerns (AI trust, governance, data, distributed consistency).

## Practical CTA ideas

- "Which quality dimension is hardest to operationalize in your context?"
- "Which standards mapping would you like next?"
- "Want to contribute examples or corrections? Join the repo discussion."
