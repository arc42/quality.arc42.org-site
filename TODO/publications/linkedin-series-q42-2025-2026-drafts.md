# LinkedIn Drafts: q42 Updates (Aug 2025 - Feb 2026)

## Post 1 - Why this series

Over the last months, we made substantial updates to **q42** (the quality model on quality.arc42.org).  
Why this matters: quality models only help when teams can actually use them in daily decisions.

In this short series, I will share the most relevant changes from Aug 2025 until now:
- clearer terminology
- better graph navigation
- stronger requirement examples
- much broader standards coverage

And yes: **q42 is open source** and part of the broader **arc42 family**.

If you work with architecture and quality goals, this series is for you.

---

## Post 2 - Terminology reset: "Dimensions" instead of "Properties"

One important q42 change: we renamed "properties" to **dimensions**.

Why this is important:
- "dimension" is semantically clearer for communication with teams/stakeholders
- less ambiguity in workshops and reviews
- aligns better with the model visualization (3-axis view)

Looks small, but language quality drives model adoption.

q42 stays open source, so this was discussed and refined transparently in the repo.

---

## Post 3 - Graph UX: from visual toy to working tool

We improved the q42 graph significantly:
- better filtering behavior
- clearer node toggles
- better focus/centering controls
- visual highlighting of nodes that actually match filters

Why this matters:
Teams use the graph to move from abstract quality words to actionable requirements/standards.  
If navigation is hard, people stop using it.

Good architecture knowledge representation needs good interaction design.

---

## Post 4 - New homepage: "Explore quality three ways"

The homepage now offers three explicit entry points:
1. focused graph
2. full graph explorer
3. textual navigation

Plus quick counters/links for qualities, requirements, standards, and approaches.

Why this matters:
- faster onboarding for first-time visitors
- lower cognitive load
- clearer "where do I start?" for different personas

This is a practical product lesson: information architecture is part of quality engineering.

---

## Post 5 - Aliases and canonical language

q42 now has a dedicated aliases/synonyms concept:
- canonical terms
- alias redirects
- an alias overview page for discoverability

Why this matters:
- reduces terminology fragmentation across teams
- improves search/discovery
- avoids duplicate concepts with different names

In architecture work, naming is not cosmetic - it is operational.

---

## Post 6 - Requirement quality uplift

We invested heavily in requirement examples:
- stronger structure and consistency
- more measurable acceptance criteria
- better practical applicability
- additional examples for modern qualities (e.g., observability, coupling, transaction consistency, updateability)

Why this matters:
"Quality" fails when it remains abstract.  
Concrete, measurable scenarios make quality discussable and testable.

q42 now contains a noticeably stronger requirement corpus for reuse by development teams.

---

## Post 7 - Standards coverage: 5 -> 35

A big step: standards coverage grew from **5 to 35**.

Added/expanded examples include ISO/IEC, ISO/IEC/IEEE, IEC, EN/ETSI, and sector-specific standards.

Why this matters:
- easier bridge between quality goals and compliance obligations
- better guidance for teams that know the domain but not all standard numbers
- more confidence in audits and regulated contexts

This is where open architecture knowledge meets real-world constraints.

---

## Post 8 - Standards UX: category view + explorer

Standards are now easier to navigate:
- category-based browsing
- searchable standards explorer
- better presentation and contextual summaries

Why this matters:
Most engineers do not memorize standard IDs.  
They need "find by intent" and "find by category" workflows.

This update improves usability without dumbing content down.

---

## Post 9 - Open source + arc42 ecosystem: what’s next

q42 is part of the **arc42 family** and developed in the open.

What this enables:
- transparent evolution (issues, PRs, discussions)
- community feedback loops
- fast correction and refinement

What’s next:
- continue improving standards mappings
- add more high-quality requirement examples
- refine navigation and model consistency

If you use arc42/q42 in practice and want to contribute use cases or corrections, I’d love to connect.
