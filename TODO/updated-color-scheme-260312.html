Color Scheme: Dimension Metaclass IMPLEMENTED

Implemented: 2026-03-12  ·  Option C — Slate Navy selected  ·  Introduces a dedicated Dimension page type (was "Characteristic"), differentiated from quality characteristics by Slate Navy (#1A3A5C / #C8E6F5).

1 — Why a new color?

The visual cue for page types currently shows "Characteristic" on tag/dimension pages because they share the property node type internally. Once we introduce a Dimension metaclass:

  • Dimensions = the top-level grouping categories (e.g. Reliable, Operable) — currently called property in the graph
  • Characteristics = individual quality attributes belonging to a dimension (e.g. Accessibility, Fault Tolerance) — currently called quality in the graph

Both live in the blue family today (#00B8F5 for characteristics, #f8f9fa near-white for dimension graph nodes). The proposal: give Dimensions a dark blue treatment so they read as "heavier / more foundational", while Characteristics keep the existing bright cyan.

3 — Three candidate Dimension colors

All three use a dark blue family with light text for legibility. Compare side-by-side:

Strongest contrast. Very dark, authoritative feel. Maximum separation from #00B8F5 cyan. Risk: can look heavy if used in large areas.
Option B — Dark Teal candidate 
Background: #0E5C6E
Text:       #D4F1F9
Bridges teal header (#357360) and quality cyan. Feels related but clearly distinct. Good harmony with the green header.
Option C — Slate Navy candidate 
Background: #1A3A5C
Text:       #C8E6F5
Middle ground: darker than teal, less extreme than deep navy. Very readable. Pairs well with all current content colors.

7 — Full updated palette with recommended choice

Reasons:

  • Visually "heavy" enough to signal top-level category without being as stark as deep navy

  • Harmonises with the existing blue-teal family (#357360 header, #00B8F5 qualities)
  • Clearly distinct from characteristics in both chips and graph nodes
  • Works well as a dark-filled graph node at size 35
